# Social Matching Device and Mobile App for International Students

## Project Overview

This project is a human-centered product design project that explores how to help university students, especially international students and students studying away from home, reduce loneliness and build more meaningful social connections.

The project proposes a hybrid social matching system that combines a portable offline matching device with a mobile app. The system is designed around a complete social journey: helping users discover people with similar interests, initiate low-pressure interaction, organize shared activities, and gradually deepen relationships.

Instead of relying only on online profiles or direct face-to-face conversation, this project creates a bridge between offline campus encounters and digital social support. The physical device helps users recognize potential matches in real-world environments, while the mobile app supports profile management, recommendation, planning, friend connection, and activity organization.

## Background and Motivation

International students often face social isolation after entering a new cultural and academic environment. Based on background research, user interviews, online survey insights, and scenario analysis, this project identified several major challenges:

- Difficulty adapting to a new cultural environment
- Language barriers in daily social interaction
- Limited opportunities to meet like-minded people
- High pressure from study and life routines
- Lack of low-pressure ways to start conversations
- Difficulty maintaining long-term relationships after the first interaction

The project therefore focuses on designing a more natural and inclusive social matching experience that supports both extroverted and introverted users.

## Target Users

The primary target users include:

- International students
- University students studying away from home
- Students who feel lonely or socially disconnected
- Students who want to meet people with similar hobbies
- Introverted users who prefer indirect and low-pressure social interaction before face-to-face communication

## Design Goal

The main design goal is to help students move from passive loneliness to active social connection.

The product is designed around the following social path:

1. **Recognize personal interests and social needs**
2. **Discover people with similar hobby or personality patterns**
3. **Start interaction through low-pressure offline cues**
4. **Connect through the mobile app**
5. **Organize shared activities**
6. **Deepen friendship through repeated social engagement**

## Product Concept

The final product concept consists of two connected parts:

### 1. Portable Social Matching Device

The physical device is a small circular device that can be attached to a backpack or carried as a personal social signal. It displays simplified hobby and personality-related icons and uses visual feedback to help users recognize people with shared interests nearby.

The purpose of the device is not to force direct communication, but to create a natural opportunity for users to notice compatibility before starting a conversation. This makes the interaction especially suitable for users who feel nervous about approaching strangers directly.

### 2. Mobile App

The mobile app acts as the digital control and relationship-building layer of the system. Users can set their hobby list, view recommended users and activities, manage friends, arrange plans, explore social opportunities, and present their personal profile.

The app also connects with the physical device, allowing users to control what information is displayed and continue interaction after an offline encounter.

## Core Matching Mechanism

### Five-Dimensional Personality Table

This project introduces a five-dimensional personality table to support a more structured matching mechanism. The five dimensions are:

- **Expressiveness**
- **Energy**
- **Orderliness**
- **Intellect**
- **Empathy**

Each hobby is assigned a five-dimensional personality score. These scores are then averaged to generate a user personality vector based on the user’s selected hobbies.

### Similarity Calculation

To calculate compatibility between users, the system compares the user vectors and animal-based personality vectors using cosine similarity. This creates a clearer and more stable matching logic than simple tag-based matching.

The use of visual animal symbols also makes the matching result easier to understand, more memorable, and more engaging for users.

## Key Features

### Interest-Informed Personality Matching

Users select hobbies and preferences in the app. The system then converts these hobbies into a five-dimensional personality profile and uses similarity calculation to recommend compatible people.

### Offline Recognition and Ice-Breaking

The physical device allows users to notice shared hobbies or compatible personality traits when passing by others in campus environments. This helps reduce the awkwardness of starting a conversation.

### Hobby Recording

The app can record people with similar hobbies who pass by the user through the device interaction. This allows users to review potential connections later instead of needing to act immediately.

### Friend Connection

Users can add friends through the app and maintain connections after offline encounters. The system supports continued communication and relationship development.

### Activity Recommendation

The app recommends activities based on the user’s interests, existing friends, and potential social opportunities. This helps users move from simple matching to real shared experiences.

### Planner

The planner feature helps users arrange activities with friends, making the product not only a matching tool but also a relationship-building tool.

### Exploration Page

The exploration section allows users to discover potential matches, social opportunities, and activity recommendations beyond their existing friend list.

### User Profile

The profile page presents the user’s hobbies, personality-related information, and activity records, helping others understand them before deeper interaction.

### QR Code Scanner

The app includes a QR code scanning function that can be used for adding friends or connecting with activities, making the interaction process faster and easier.

## User Scenario

A typical use scenario is:

1. A student feels lonely and wants to make new friends but does not know how to start.
2. The student connects the physical device with the mobile app.
3. The student sets their hobby list and personal preferences.
4. When another user with shared hobbies passes by, the device provides a subtle visual signal.
5. The user can check the app to view the potential match.
6. The users can connect, arrange an activity, and gradually deepen their friendship.

This scenario supports a smooth transition from offline discovery to online connection and then to real social activity.

## Design Process

### 1. Background Research

The project began with research on loneliness among international students, including causes, effects, and common social barriers.

Research topics included:

- International student population trends
- Cultural adaptation difficulties
- Language barriers
- Lack of social support
- Psychological effects of loneliness
- Difficulties in making friends

### 2. User Research

The project conducted primary and secondary research, including:

- Interviews with international students
- Online survey analysis
- Social media discussion analysis
- Scenario analysis
- Research on social pain points and friendship barriers

These research findings helped define the target user group and the main design direction.

### 3. Persona Development

A persona was created to represent an international student who wants to make friends but finds it difficult to start social interaction. The persona helped guide the product toward low-pressure interaction, hobby-based matching, and activity-driven relationship building.

### 4. Matching System Design

The project designed a five-dimensional personality table and transformed hobby information into structured personality scores. This mechanism was used to support more meaningful user matching and reduce the randomness of social discovery.

### 5. Physical Device Design

Multiple device forms were sketched and tested. The design process considered:

- Hand comfort
- Backpack hanging method
- Portability
- Visibility
- Interaction naturalness
- Screen readability
- Visual style

After prototype testing, the final device form was selected because it was more comfortable to hold, more natural to attach to a backpack, and better suited for everyday campus use.

### 6. App Information Architecture

The app structure includes:

- Recommendation
- Planner
- Friend List
- Exploration
- User Profile
- QR Code Scanner

This structure supports both immediate social discovery and long-term relationship management.

### 7. Interface Design

Low-fidelity and high-fidelity app prototypes were created to explore page layout, interaction flow, visual hierarchy, and content structure.

### 8. Prototype Testing and Iteration

Physical prototype testing was conducted to collect feedback on comfort, portability, and interaction experience. The design was then iterated based on user feedback, especially around how the device should be carried and how naturally it could support offline social recognition.
