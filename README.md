# Social Matching Device and Mobile App for University Students

## Project Overview

This project is a human-centered product design project that explores how to help university students, especially international students and students studying away from home, overcome loneliness, limited social opportunities, and the difficulty of finding like-minded peers.

The project proposes a hybrid social matching solution that combines a physical social matching device with a mobile app. Instead of relying only on online chatting or traditional profile-based matching, the system is designed to support a complete offline-to-online social journey: discovering people with similar interests, initiating low-pressure interaction, building connections, organizing activities, and deepening relationships over time.

Through user interviews, questionnaires, secondary research, scenario analysis, interface prototyping, and physical product modeling, this project developed a complete product concept that integrates user research, interaction design, visual design, device form exploration, and social matching mechanism design.

## Problem Statement

Many university students face social challenges after entering a new campus environment. These challenges are especially common among international students and students studying away from home, who may experience:

- A lack of familiar social networks
- Difficulty finding people with similar interests
- Limited opportunities for natural offline interaction
- Social pressure when initiating conversations with strangers
- A gap between online social platforms and real-world campus interaction

This project aims to reduce the friction of making new friends by creating a product experience that makes shared interests more visible, approachable, and actionable in offline campus settings.

## Target Users

The primary target users include:

- University students
- International students
- Students studying away from home
- Students who feel lonely or socially disconnected
- Students who want to find peers with similar interests
- Introverted users who prefer low-pressure ways to learn about others before direct face-to-face interaction

## Design Goal

The main design goal is to create a social matching system that helps users move from interest discovery to meaningful connection.

The product is designed around the following social path:

1. **Discover similar people**  
   Help users identify people with shared interests or compatible preferences in offline campus environments.

2. **Start a connection naturally**  
   Reduce the awkwardness of initiating conversations by providing visible and lightweight social cues.

3. **Organize shared activities**  
   Recommend activities based on users’ interests and social needs.

4. **Deepen relationships**  
   Support continued interaction through friend connections, personal profiles, and activity-based engagement.

## Product Concept

The final concept consists of two connected components:

### 1. Physical Social Matching Device

The device is designed as a portable or wearable object that helps users recognize potential social matches in real-world environments.

Its purpose is not to replace direct conversation, but to create a more natural opportunity for users to notice shared interests and start interactions. The physical device supports instant recognition, social signaling, and low-pressure ice-breaking in offline scenarios.

### 2. Mobile App

The mobile app provides the digital layer of the system. It supports profile presentation, interest management, friend connection, activity recommendation, and social matching.

The app allows users to express who they are, explore potential matches, and continue interactions after the initial offline encounter.

## Key Features

### Interest-Based Matching

The system matches users based on shared interests and preference similarity. Instead of relying only on basic profile information, it uses a structured interest classification method to support more meaningful matching.

### Five-Dimensional Interest Classification Framework

To improve matching quality, this project introduces an original five-dimensional interest classification framework. The framework is used to calculate users’ interest similarity and create a more stable matching mechanism.

This design helps the system move beyond random social discovery and provides a clearer basis for recommending compatible users.

### Offline Ice-Breaking Interaction

The physical device is designed to help users recognize potential matches in offline environments and reduce the pressure of starting a conversation.

This is especially important for introverted users, who may want to learn about another person’s interests before direct interaction.

### Activity Recommendation

The app supports activity recommendations based on shared interests, helping users move from initial matching to real social participation.

### Personal Profile Presentation

Users can build personal profiles to present their interests, personality, and activity preferences, making it easier for others to understand them before starting a conversation.

### Inclusive Interaction for Introverted Users

The product specifically considers users with different personalities and social preferences. For introverted users, the system provides ways to understand others without requiring immediate face-to-face interaction.

## Design Process

### 1. User Research

The project began with primary and secondary research to understand students’ social pain points.

Research methods included:

- Interviews with international students
- Questionnaire research
- Social media and news analysis
- Academic article review
- Scenario analysis of campus social interaction

These research activities helped define the target user group, core problem space, and product direction.

### 2. Product Definition

Based on the research findings, the project defined the product around a complete social journey:

**interest discovery → initial connection → activity organization → relationship development**

This structure helped ensure that the product was not only a matching tool, but also a system that supports the full process of building social relationships.

### 3. Interaction and Interface Design

The mobile app interface was designed to support interest matching, activity discovery, profile browsing, and friend connection.

Both low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes were created to explore the app structure, user flow, and visual presentation.

### 4. Device Form Design

The physical device was designed and modeled to support portable offline interaction.

Tools used in the design process included:

- **Procreate** for concept sketching and visual exploration
- **Figma** for app interface design and prototyping
- **Blender** for device modeling and rendering

### 5. Prototype Testing and Iteration

A physical product model was created and tested with users. Feedback was collected on:

- Hand comfort
- Portability
- Interaction naturalness
- Visual style
- Ease of use in offline social situations

Based on user feedback, the product form and interaction details were iterated multiple times.
